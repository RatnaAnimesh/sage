# 3. Thermodynamic Inference and Dynamic Causality

To function as a generalized intelligence, an agent must construct rigorous predictions, handle incomplete information, and continuously intervene in the environment. SAGE unifies these operational directives by synthesizing thermodynamic biological modeling with probabilistic, non-axiomatic logic.

## 3.1 Non-Axiomatic Logic (NAL) and Reasoning Under Uncertainty

In unstructured physical environments, absolute certainty is unachievable; truth is necessarily bounded by the volume of empirical data. SAGE implements Pei Wang’s **Non-Axiomatic Reasoning System (NARS)**, assigning a fundamental mathematical tuple `<Frequency, Confidence>` to every logical Morphism bounding a relationship.

- **Frequency ($f$)**: The ratio of positive evidence to total evidence ($f = w^+ / w$).
- **Confidence ($c$)**: The mapped volume of accumulated evidence defining the epistemic weight of the conclusion ($c = w / (w + k)$).

Deduction, abduction, and induction are conducted through stringent non-axiomatic formulations (e.g., $c_{deduction} = c_1 \cdot c_2 \cdot f_1 \cdot f_2$). These multiplicative limits guarantee that generated hypotheses are strictly bounded, preventing the unchecked confidence inflation—and corresponding hallucination—endemic to connectionist models.

## 3.2 The Variational Free Energy Principle and Active Inference

SAGE is not an inert database awaiting a prompt. It operates as an autonomous biological unit motivated singularly by Karl Friston’s **Variational Free Energy Principle**. SAGE interprets thermodynamic Surprise as identical to Information Theoretic prediction error: the divergence between its internal generative representation and its physical sensory perceptions.

To minimize an upper boundary on this Surprise ($\mathcal{F}$), the inference engine operates on a continuous, decentralized two-step phase loop:
1. **Perceptual Inference (Model Updating):** SAGE adjusts internal topological arrangements to map physical reality precisely, abducting environmental states.
2. **Active Inference (World Intervention):** SAGE dictates actions that minimize *Expected Free Energy* (a composite of epistemic ambiguity and instrumental risk), coercing the external environment into alignment with its homeostatic models.

## 3.3 Dynamic Operator Theory and Continuous Do-Calculus

Active Inference requires predictive causal simulations. SAGE structures reality into a generalized **Structural Causal Model (SCM)**, granting the capability for active geometric intervention via Judea Pearl’s **Do-Calculus** ($do(X=x)$).

However, real-time continuous non-linear chaotic dynamics are exceptionally mathematically dense, traditionally restricting Do-Calculus to fixed or mock probabilities. SAGE overcomes this computational barrier by employing **Koopman Operator Theory**. By formally lifting the non-linear environment into an infinite-dimensional linear space of observables, SAGE successfully linearizes highly complex dynamical systems into scalable matrices. 

When SAGE simulates a $do()$ intervention, the counterfactual seamlessly propagates through a linear Koopman matrix. When physical, real-world consequences dictate a structural divergence from the internal model (Surprise spikes), the architecture dynamically implements **Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs)** to recalculate and overwrite the governing SCM interaction laws via free energy gradient descent. In this manner, SAGE literally dynamically discovers and internalizes the fundamental structural mechanics of its environment without human mediation.
