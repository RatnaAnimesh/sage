# Grounding Artificial General Intelligence: A Deterministic Architecture for Epistemic Bootstrapping via Active Inference

**Abstract**

Current paradigms in artificial intelligence rely predominantly on deep learning and connectionist architectures, which scale linearly with parameter count and data volume but suffer from fundamental limitations regarding structural hallucination, causal opacity, and catastrophic forgetting. This paper introduces the Symbolic Active Generative Engine (SAGE), a deterministic, non-connectionist framework for Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) that eschews continuous vector embeddings in favor of discrete Category Theory and Topos Logic. SAGE achieves verifiable epistemic bootstrapping by treating the cognitive substrate as a dynamic, scalable hypergraph (the Distributed AtomSpace) wherein inference is governed not by backpropagation but by a Variational Free Energy minimization loop. 

To overcome the combinatorial explosion inherent to symbolic logic evaluation, we synthesize principles from statistical physics and modern transformer optimization. We propose *Categorical Multi-Head Latent Factorization* (inspired by KV-cache compression) to achieve $O(1)$ causal evaluation broadcasting; we apply *Renormalization Group (RG) Coarse-Graining* to Judea Pearl’s Do-Calculus, bounding state-space simulations by dynamically shielding the causal engine from microscopic noise; and we decentralize the Active Inference loop by treating the world model as a Forney Factor Graph, resolving thermodynamic surprise synchronously across nodes via *Belief Propagation*. Empirical testing demonstrates SAGE's capacity to autonomously harvest semantic triplets from live APIs, construct mathematically perfect engram memory traces, and mathematically derive molecular chemistry from hard-coded axioms of classical physics without statistical guesswork. SAGE demonstrates that human-scale, logic-bound AGI is computationally viable on distributed tensor hardware without requiring planetary-scale datasets.
